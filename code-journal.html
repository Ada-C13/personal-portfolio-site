My Notes about Code

My writings and musings about code and the dev life.

Learning about HTML & CSS: flexbox & grid

I first started building webpages almost 25 years ago in plain notepad. People were still getting online with AOL, and the blink tag was popular with people my age. Styling HTML has come a long way since then.

While building this site, I've been learning about the box model for designing websites and the differences between flexbox and grid for layouts.

Some of the questions that have come up for me:

  • Can you combine flexbox and grid?
  • What are the differences between flexbox and grid?
  • Is it still OK to use div?
  • Why does it break my layout when I make a column or row have a fixed position on the page?

Adjusting to online learning in the times of Covid

I've worked in tech for a long time and I'm no stranger to work-from-home days, but this situation has been a whole new one. Not only am I learning new complicated material, sharing a space with a partner 24/7, but I'm also adjusting to some scary things happening in the world.

So how do I adjust?

Some of my coping strategies have included:

  • Asking people who work remote full-time for tips
  • Setting up a specific work space
  • Cleaning and organizing the area around my desk
  • Working out systems with my partner to specify heads-down time
  • Setting a transition for when I go to work: I go for a walk before starting work
  • Reaching out to classmates and offering help where I can, and asking for it where I need it
  • Getting a second monitor setup
